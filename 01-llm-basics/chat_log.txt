### Transformer Architecture in Large Language Models (LLMs)

#### 1. **Overview:**
Transformers are a neural network architecture primarily used for sequential data tasks, especially natural language processing (NLP). LLMs like GPT, BERT, and their variants rely on transformers due to their ability to model long-range dependencies efficiently.

---

#### 2. **Core Components:**

- **Input Embedding:**  
  Converts input tokens (words/subwords) into continuous vector representations.

- **Positional Encoding:**  
  Since transformers lack recurrence, positional encodings add information about the token positions in the sequence.

- **Multi-Head Self-Attention (MHSA):**  
  Allows the model to attend to different parts of the input sequence simultaneously, capturing context from multiple representation subspaces.

- **Feed-Forward Neural Network (FFN):**  
  A position-wise fully connected network applied independently to each token, introducing non-linearity and complexity.

- **Layer Normalization & Residual Connections:**  
  Applied around attention and feed-forward sublayers to stabilize training and enable deeper networks.

---

#### 3. **Architecture Details:**

- **Input Sequence:**  
  \( \mathbf{X} = [x_1, x_2, ..., x_n] \) where \( n \) is sequence length.

- **Embedding + Positional Encoding:**  
  \( \mathbf{E} = \text{Embedding}(\mathbf{X}) + \text{PosEnc}(n, d) \)  
  \( d \) is the embedding dimension.

- **Self-Attention Mechanism:**

  For each token:
  - Compute queries \( Q \), keys \( K \), and values \( V \) via learned linear projections:  
    \( Q = \mathbf{E}W_Q, \quad K = \mathbf{E}W_K, \quad V = \mathbf{E}W_V \)
  - Attention scores:  
    \( \text{Attention}(Q,K,V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V \)  
    where \( d_k \) is the dimensionality of the keys.

- **Multi-Head Self-Attention:**

  Instead of one attention mechanism, use \( h \) parallel attention heads to capture diverse patterns:  
  \[
  \text{MHSA}(\mathbf{E}) = \text{Concat}(\text{head}_1, ..., \text{head}_h)W_O
  \]
  where each \( \text{head}_i = \text{Attention}(Q_i,K_i,V_i) \).

- **Position-wise Feed-Forward Network (FFN):**  
  Applied independently to each token embedding:  
  \[
  \text{FFN}(x) = \max(0, xW_1 + b_1)W_2 + b_2
  \]

---

#### 4. **Layer Structure:**

Each Transformer layer consists of:

1. **MHSA sublayer:**  
   Input → LayerNorm → MHSA → Add & Residual Connection

2. **FFN sublayer:**  
   Result → LayerNorm → FFN → Add & Residual Connection

---

#### 5. **Stacking Layers:**

The transformer encoder or decoder stacks \( N \) such layers (typically 12, 24, or more layers in LLMs).

---

#### 6. **Decoder vs. Encoder:**

- **Encoder Transformer:**  
  All tokens attend to each other (bidirectional). Used in BERT.

- **Decoder Transformer:**  
  Uses masked attention, preventing tokens from attending to future tokens (causal masking). Used in GPT and autoregressive LLMs.

---

#### 7. **Output:**

- The final layer's output is used for downstream tasks:  
  - **Language Modeling:** Predict next token via softmax over vocabulary.  
  - **Classification/QA:** Additional heads on top of transformer outputs.
